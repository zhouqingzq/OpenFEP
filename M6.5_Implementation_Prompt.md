# OpenFEP M6.5 Implementation Prompt

## Milestone

M6.5: Meta-Control Guidance

## Goal

Generate deterministic, auditable meta-control guidance from `CognitiveStateMVP`, affective state, path competition, diagnostics, and prior outcomes. The guidance should affect prompt conditioning first, not core policy scoring.

## Work Principles

- Meta-control is a slow guidance signal, not a homunculus or second policy engine.
- Guidance must be deterministic for the same trace.
- First integration target is prompt/generation conditioning.
- Guidance must avoid over-interpreting hidden intent.

def generate_meta_control_guidance(
    state: CognitiveStateMVP,
    *,
    diagnostics: DecisionDiagnostics | None = None,

    previous_outcome: str = "",

Triggers:
- low policy or EFE margin
- high conflict tension
- low social safety or high repair need
- elevated irritation or arousal
- repeated negative outcome
- memory conflict
- prompt overload
- high hidden intent with low observability
- identity or commitment tension

### 3. Integrate as diagnostics only

Expose guidance in:
- turn trace
- generation diagnostics
- M6.6 prompt capsule input
- dashboard-readable Chinese summaries of affective maintenance
- session `Conscious.md` summaries

Do not change policy selection in M6.5.

## Boundary Constraints

- Do not mutate `ranked_options`, `policy_score`, or chosen action.
- Do not automatically transform high hidden-intent score into suspicion.
- Do not replace `MetaCognitiveLayer`; reuse its outputs where useful.
- Do not add stochastic exploration here.
- Do not let guidance bypass safety or governance layers.
- Do not let affective guidance accuse the user, perform sentiment certainty claims, or override the selected action.
- Do not write meta-control guidance into `Self-consciousness.md` except through a later slow-consolidation step.

- high hidden intent alone sets avoid-overinterpretation, not paranoia
- prior failure increases repair or caution guidance
- guidance does not change selected action

## Acceptance Criteria

- Same inputs produce same guidance.
- Low-confidence decisions produce lower assertion or clarification guidance.
- High conflict can produce repair-oriented guidance.
- Affective maintenance can produce deescalation, warmth preservation, or intensity reduction guidance.
- High hidden intent does not automatically produce suspicious interpretation.
- Guidance appears in trace and generation diagnostics.
- Guidance appears in session conscious artifacts without becoming a source of truth.
- Core policy behavior remains unchanged.
